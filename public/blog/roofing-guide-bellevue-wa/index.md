# Complete Roofing Guide for Bellevue, WA Homeowners

*Everything Bellevue homeowners need to know about roof replacement, repair, and maintenance. Local weather challenges, material recommendations, and what to expect.*

## Roofing in Bellevue: What Every Homeowner Should Know

Bellevue is one of the most desirable cities on the Eastside, with home values among the highest in Washington State. Protecting your investment starts with your roof — and Bellevue's climate presents unique challenges that homeowners need to plan for.

### Bellevue's Climate and Your Roof

Bellevue averages over 37 inches of rain per year, with most falling between October and May. The city's mix of waterfront properties near Lake Washington and hillside homes in neighborhoods like Somerset and Cougar Mountain means roofing needs vary significantly across the city.

**Common Bellevue roofing challenges:**
- **Heavy rainfall** — persistent moisture puts constant stress on roofing systems
- **Tree coverage** — Bellevue's lush tree canopy drops needles and leaves, clogging gutters and promoting moss growth
- **Wind exposure** — homes on hillsides and near the lake face stronger wind events
- **Moss and algae** — shaded neighborhoods like Bridle Trails see aggressive moss growth

### How Often Should Bellevue Homeowners Inspect Their Roof?

We recommend a professional inspection every 2–3 years, or immediately after a major storm. Bellevue homeowners should also have their roof cleaned every 2–3 years to prevent moss damage — especially in tree-heavy neighborhoods like Enatai, Woodridge, and Bridle Trails.
