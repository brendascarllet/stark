Kirkland Roofing Questions

How much does it cost to replace a cedar shake roof in Kirkland?

Cedar shake tear-offs cost more than a standard shingle removal because the material is heavier and the deck underneath almost always needs work. A typical Kirkland home with cedar shake runs $15,000 to $28,000 for a full conversion to GAF architectural shingles. That includes new plywood sheathing over the old skip boards, ice and water shield, and the full GAF system.

Will composite shingles look as good as my old cedar shake?

GAF Grand Sequoia shingles have a thick, dimensional profile that gives you a similar layered look to cedar without the splitting and moss problems. We bring samples to every estimate so you can hold them against your home's siding and trim. Most homeowners are surprised at how close the match is, and they like that the new roof won't need treatment every few years.

The wind off Lake Washington tore shingles off my roof. Will this keep happening?

If your current shingles are 3-tab style, yes. 3-tab shingles have a single layer with more surface area for wind to catch. GAF HDZ architectural shingles are rated to 130 mph when installed with the full system. They're thicker, heavier, and the adhesive strips hold better. Proper nailing pattern matters too. Six nails per shingle in high-wind areas, not four.

How long does a roof replacement take in Kirkland?

Most Kirkland homes take 2 to 4 days. Cedar shake tear-offs can add a day because the material removal is slower and we're usually replacing sheathing underneath. Steep roofs and limited access on hillside lots can also add time. We give you the timeline in the estimate, and we update you if anything changes once we open up the roof.
