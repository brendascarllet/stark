Issaquah Roofing Questions

Does the heavier rain in Issaquah mean I need a different type of shingle?

Not a different shingle, but a better installation. We use ice and water shield in every valley and around every penetration, not just at the eaves. GAF HDZ shingles handle the rain fine. The failures we see in Issaquah come from shortcuts in the underlayment and flashing, not the shingles themselves.

Our roof has storm damage from a windstorm. Can you help with the insurance claim?

We document the damage with photos and measurements, and we'll meet with your adjuster on-site if needed. We don't file claims for you, but we give you everything you need to file yours. Most adjusters in King County know our work, and that makes the process smoother.

How do I know if the moss on my roof is actually causing damage?

Thin surface moss can be treated and removed without replacing the roof. Thick moss that's lifting shingle tabs is a different story. Once shingle edges lift, water runs under them with every rain. I'll check the shingle condition underneath the moss during the estimate and show you photos so you can see what I see.

Can you replace a roof in Issaquah during the winter?

Yes. We roof through the winter as long as conditions allow safe work. GAF shingles install fine in cold weather. We plan around the forecast and won't start a tear-off if heavy rain is coming the next day. Most of our winter jobs go smoothly because we schedule tighter and keep crew sizes up.
